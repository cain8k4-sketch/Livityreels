🌿 THE ITAL WAY – FOOD & DRINK

ITAL IS VITAL!

What is Ital?
Ital is the natural, life-giving way of eating in Rastafari. It is locally grown, strictly organic, and comes directly from the earth — fresh fruits, vegetables, herbs, grains, and coconut. Ital rejects industrial farming, chemical pesticides, and processed foods. It is prepared with love and respect for the body as the temple of Jah. No added sugar, no artificial flavours, no preservatives. It keeps the body clean, strong, and in perfect balance with nature and Jah.

🍹 Ital Drinks (No Added Sugar)

Ginger Beer
Fresh ginger, lemon, water, a touch of honey or dates (optional). Blend, strain, serve cold.
Sorrel Drink
Dried sorrel (hibiscus), ginger, cinnamon, cloves, water. Steep overnight, strain, serve chilled.
Pineapple & Coconut Water
Fresh pineapple blended with pure coconut water. Refreshing and full of enzymes.

🍲 Ital Foods (No Added Sugar)

Ital Vegetable Stew
Pumpkin, carrot, potato, okra, callaloo, thyme, garlic, scotch bonnet, coconut milk. Simmer slow.
Ackee & Callaloo
Sauté callaloo with onion, garlic, tomato, scotch bonnet. Add ackee at the end. Serve with roasted breadfruit.
Coconut Run Down
Coconut milk reduced with pumpkin, carrot, green banana, okra, and herbs. Rich and satisfying.

❤️💛💚 THE FOUNDER & HIS MESSAGE

Marcus Mosiah Garvey (1887 – 1940)

The Prophet • The Visionary • The Father of Black Nationalism

Marcus Garvey was a Jamaican-born visionary who founded the Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA). He is revered in Rastafari as the great prophet who prepared the way for the return of the Messiah.

His Sacred Message to the World:

  • “Africa for the Africans, at home and abroad!” — The call for Black self-determination and repatriation.
  • “Look to the East for the crowning of a Black King” — The prophecy fulfilled in 1930 with the coronation of Emperor Haile Selassie I.
  • “Emancipate yourselves from mental slavery” — A call for mental and spiritual liberation (later immortalised by Bob Marley).
  • Black pride, self-reliance, and economic independence — “Up, you mighty race, you can accomplish what you will!”

Garvey’s vision of Black dignity, African redemption, and the coming of a Black Messiah laid the spiritual and ideological foundation for the Rastafari movement. His words continue to guide I & I today.
